What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals pretend to be trusted organizations to steal passwords, banking information, or personal data.

How to Recognize Phishing Emails

  • ❌ Unknown sender
  • ❌ Urgent messages
  • ❌ Spelling mistakes
  • ❌ Suspicious links
  • ❌ Unexpected attachments

Fake Website Warning Signs

  • URL spelling mistakes
  • No HTTPS lock icon
  • Poor website design
  • Unexpected login page
  • Requests for sensitive information

Social Engineering Techniques

Technique Description
Phishing Fake emails requesting login credentials.
Smishing Fraud through SMS messages.
Vishing Phone calls pretending to be banks or companies.
Baiting Offering free gifts or USB drives.

Best Practices

  1. Verify email sender.
  2. Never click suspicious links.
  3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication.
  4. Use strong passwords.
  5. Keep software updated.
  6. Report suspicious emails.

Real-World Example

Fake Bank Email

Subject: Your Account Will Be Closed!

Dear Customer,

Your account has been suspended. Click below immediately.

www.bank-security-login.com

⚠ Warning: Fake domain, urgent message, suspicious link.
